Airbnb Office

The AirBnB Home Office is a new business segment that leverages the existing "Home away from home" community that AirBnB has built as an organization. This relationship has established trust and we felt the Home Office booking would be easily integrated into their existing business model, since the infrastructure already exist.

Duration
3 Weeks
Tools Used
Figma, Miro, InVision
Collaborators
Parastou S, Yanni L, Tasha C

Part 1: Research

We collected quantitative and qualitative
data by performing 6 one-on-one interviews
and surveying 20 people through Facebook
surveys.

Part 2: Defining the "Why"

We started the affinity diagram by putting
each point that the interviewees made on
asticky notes and assigned each person a
specific colour.

Problem Statement:

As a result of the pandemic, working from
home has become the new normal. This has
resulted, in individuals having a hard time
adapting. Challenges such as distractions
and a volitile working culture contributes to
the need for external spaces that enable
collaboration to meet the ever changing
demands of the world of work and study.

How might we provide practical, convenient
and fresh solutions and opportunities for
individuals struggling to work from home to
achieve their work/study goals?

Part 3: Ideation

We used the "I Like, I Wish, What If" method
in order to ideate possible solutions to help
our users achieve their goals and needs as
well as minimize their problems .

After discussing and voting in the I like, I
wish and what if stage, we created the
feature prioritization matrix which helped
us come up with specific features such
specific filters, discount deals, and unique
offices which you will see in the prototypes.

Having foung good enough solutions we
decided to create a storyboard to give out
solution a personality.

Part 4 & 5: Prototyping and Testing

We started by sketching the user flow from opening the airbnb app to booking a home office to a complete. We wanted to give user the ability to choose from a list of home offices that they searched for, as well as finding unique offices and vip deals. From there we each sketched our own ideas into paper wireframes. We believe this is a really productive method as we can quickly sketch our ideas and iterate. We voted on the wireframes that worked best with our feature on Airbnb and moved on to sketching low-fidelity digital wireframes.

After a few rounds of testing, we made some iterations based on the feedback we recieved. Here are the changes and final look!

1. We made our feature product the center of the homepage as Airbnb usally does with every new feature they offer. This way the user is able to spot it sooner and click on it to find out more. 2. We maximized the use of white space by cleaning up the information the user actually needed to see. 3. We cleaned up the layout and made it more presentable to the user and familiar with what they usally see on Airbnb. We also added "Payment methods" as a way to speed up the booking process for users who have booked other stay with Airbnb in the past.
